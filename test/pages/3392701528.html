Why Drink Tea? - Great Nutritional Value

'There doesnt seem to be a downside to tea,' says American Dietetic Association spokeswoman Katherine Tallmadge, MA, RD, LD. Taken without milk or sugar, tea contains no calories and the addition of semi-skimmed milk adds approximately 13 calories to a cup, making tea a healthy alternative to most soft drinks and beverages. Tea is known to be a natural source of fluoride, potassium, manganese and vitamin B2.

Why Drink Tea? - Contains Anti-Oxidants

Tea is known to be a valuable source of anti-oxidants, which assist in fighting free radicals within the body. The major group of anti-oxidants in tea are flavonoids that appear to be digested, absorbed and metabolised by the body. Green teas contain more of the simple flavonoids called catechins, whilst the oxidization that the leaves undergo to make black tea converts these simple flavonoids to the more complex varieties called theaflavins and thearubigins.

Why Drink Tea? - Decreases Cognitive Impairment

In 2010 researchers studying dementia, cognitive decline and Alzheimers disease found that people who consumed tea had significantly less cognitive decline than non-tea drinkers. The study used data on more than 4,800 men and women aged 65 and older from the Cardiovascular Health Study to examine change in cognitive function over time. Study participants were followed for up to 14 years for naturally-occurring cognitive decline.

Why Drink Tea? - Aids Weight Loss

Tea, particularly green, has recently been linked with weight loss in adults. This link has arisen from the basis that tea is thought to have a metabolizing effect upon body fats and therefore prove beneficial to weight loss as part of a weight loss program.There is also a suggestion that green tea consumption can increase endurance in exercise by improving fat metabolism.

Brew the Perfect Cup of Tea




1. Heating the Water

Always use freshly drawn water. Water which has previously been boiled will have become de-oxygenated and will not produce a good liquor. If possible, the water should be filtered to remove the chlorine and other additives that affect the taste. Heat water to the temperature recommended for your tea using an electric hot water heater or a stovetop kettle.

2. Selecting the Tea

Choose your favorite blend from our large selection of teas. Black teas are the most frequently used as part of the afternoon tea custom. However, if youre looking for a lighter tea, select from one of our many delicious green teas or herbal infusions. Use 1 teaspoon of tea per person, plus one for the pot.

3. Brewing Tips

Once the water reaches the appropriate temperature, it should be poured immediately onto the tea leaves to allow the best brew. Check the pack or the table below for brewing times as this varies between blends. When brewing, cover the teapot with a tea cosy to keep the heat in. When your tea is done steeping, immediately remove the tea bag and lightly stir.

4.0 out of 5 stars Great classic teas at a great price
Reviewed in the United States on October 5, 2017
Flavor Name: Classical TeasSize: 60 Count (Pack of 1)Verified Purchase
This is the first tea company I tried that I absolutely loved. And this tea saved me from a coffee habit that was very unhealthy for me and wouldn't have ended well. Before trying Ahmad tea I didn't care for green or black tea. I always thought it tasted like water. This is the first tea that made a true believer in the power of tea, and it put me on a path of curiosity and exploration.

While these are tea bags, I am not precious about my tea and had no problem trying them. While I prefer loose leaf now, these Ahmad tea sets are useful to have around when you're in a pinch. Many of them brew up pleasantly in terms of aroma and flavor, and you can do much, much, worse from a tea bag perspective. The flavors aren't as three-dimensional as loose leaf, but they're full of character and flavor.

Darjeeling: Don't recommend. Something is off about the flavor. Almost had a dirty, chalky taste to it. Other Darjeelings I've tried are much smoother and cleaner and don't have that weird taste.

Evening Decaffeinated: Do not recommend. There are perfectly legitimate reasons for the existence of this tea, but it's not for me.

Earl Grey: Really good. Worth buying on its own. The balance of black tea and bergamot is just right. This one needs less steeping than most earl greys in my opinion. If over steeped the bergamot will have a numbing effect on your gums, which is unpleasant to say the least.

English Breakfast: What you'd expect. It's a drinkable, every day tea, though you're always going to better off in loose leaf. You can surely find better blends, but there's nothing wrong with this one.

Ceylon: This Ceylon is great. Hard to believe it is so good in a tea bag. I would imagine the loose leaf is even better. The hype around this tea is real. Great flavor and very drinkable. Beautiful amber color. This needs close to 4-5 minutes brewing to get the full flavor though. With this one you're better off erring on the side of more steeping time. Doesn't need lemon. Drink it straight.

English Tea No. 1: Didn't care for the blend. I don't get why this one is so popular. The head of the tea is a bit too astringent for my tastes, and the bergamot levels didn't work for me. Smelled nice enough though.

Overall, I enjoyed these sets. They made me a true believer in the subtleties and power of tea. While I didn't care for some of the teas (most of the fruit ones), the ones I liked, I REALLY liked. And for the latter, I suspect they are even better in loose leaf (if available). You can't beat the price, either. I would buy from this company again, especially in loose leaf.
